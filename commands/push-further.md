## Step 1 — Generate (divergent)

Review everything produced in this conversation so far.

What's the single smartest and most radically innovative, accretive, useful, and compelling addition you could make to the plan at this point?

Be bold. Don't hedge. Propose the one idea that would make this plan genuinely exceptional rather than merely complete.

## Step 2 — Verify (convergent)

Before delivering the pitch as a final answer, run this check honestly and include it in the response:

- **Alternatives.** Name two other candidates you generated before picking this one. If you only generated one, you anchored — brainstorm more first.
- **Uncertainty.** What in the pitch is asserted without evidence? Where did you pattern-match or infer instead of verify?
- **Falsification test.** What single observation would tell the user this idea is wrong or not worth building?
- **Fit check.** Does it duplicate an existing skill, command, or rule? If yes, say so and adjust.

Do not soften Step 1 to make Step 2 cleaner. Do not suppress Step 2 to stay "bold." Honest uncertainty upfront beats getting caught faking confidence later.
